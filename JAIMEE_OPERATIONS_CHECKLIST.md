# Belle Étage — Operations Checklist for Jaimee

Logan handles: website, marketing, online presence.
Everything below is Jaimee's responsibility to set up before opening.

---

## Phase 1 — Legal & Business Formation (do first, blocks everything else)

- [ ] **Choose business entity.** LLC is the default for a solo wellness brand. Talk to a CPA before picking S-corp.
- [ ] **Form the LLC** with the CT Secretary of State (~$120 filing fee). https://business.ct.gov
- [ ] **Get an EIN** from the IRS (free, takes 10 min online). Required for bank account.
- [ ] **Draft an Operating Agreement** (even as a single-member LLC — protects liability shield).
- [ ] **Register the trade name** "Belle Étage" with the town clerk if operating under that name vs. the LLC's legal name.
- [ ] **File a USPTO trademark application** for "Belle Étage" in Class 5 (supplements), Class 3 (cosmetics), and/or Class 44 (wellness services) — whichever apply. ~$350/class. Use a trademark attorney; do not DIY.
- [ ] **Check domain ownership.** Confirm belleetage.com (or chosen variant) is in her name, not Logan's.

## Phase 2 — Banking & Finance

- [ ] Open a **business checking account** (Chase, Bluevine, or Mercury). Bring EIN + LLC docs.
- [ ] Open a **business credit card** for clean expense tracking.
- [ ] Set up **bookkeeping software** (QuickBooks, Wave, or Xero).
- [ ] Hire a **CPA** who works with small product/wellness businesses in CT. Ask about quarterly estimated taxes.
- [ ] Register for **CT sales tax permit** if selling physical products (https://portal.ct.gov/DRS).

## Phase 3 — Insurance (do BEFORE selling anything)

- [ ] **General liability** — basic business coverage.
- [ ] **Product liability** — *critical* if she sells supplements, skincare, or anything ingestible/topical. Without this one bad reaction = personal bankruptcy.
- [ ] **Professional liability (E&O)** — covers advice/recommendations given as a nutrition expert.
- [ ] **Cyber liability** — if storing any customer health data or running e-commerce.
- [ ] Get quotes from Hiscox, Next Insurance, or a local CT broker.

## Phase 4 — Regulatory Compliance (wellness-specific — DO NOT SKIP)

- [ ] **FDA disclaimers on every health claim.** "These statements have not been evaluated by the FDA. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease." Required on supplements and supplement-adjacent content.
- [ ] **FTC compliance review** on all marketing copy — no unsubstantiated health claims, no fake testimonials, no before/afters without disclaimers.
- [ ] **Never call herself a doctor, pharmacist, or medical professional.** Bio = "BS in Nutrition and Chemistry, University of Arizona." (See `jaimee_credentials` memory.)
- [ ] If selling supplements, decide: **white-label/private-label** (easier) vs. **manufacturing** (FDA facility registration required).
- [ ] **Affiliate disclosure** on any product she recommends in exchange for commission (FTC requirement).
- [ ] If she takes 1-on-1 client intake info, decide whether she needs **HIPAA-aware** workflows. Nutrition consulting usually doesn't, but health intake forms can trigger state rules.
- [ ] Check **Connecticut Department of Public Health** rules on nutrition counseling — CT has restrictions on who can call themselves a "nutritionist" vs. "Registered Dietitian." Get the language right.

## Phase 5 — Business Model Decisions (she needs to commit before Phase 6)

- [ ] **What is she actually selling?** Pick one to start:
  - Curated affiliate product edits (lowest risk, lowest margin)
  - Her own branded supplements (highest margin, highest regulatory burden)
  - 1-on-1 consulting / coaching packages
  - Group programs / courses
  - Content + sponsorships
- [ ] **Pricing.** Set price tiers for whatever she chose above.
- [ ] **Fulfillment plan** if physical products: self-ship from home, or 3PL (ShipBob, ShipMonk)?
- [ ] **Returns/refund policy** in writing.

- [ ] **Payment processing** (Stripe for services; Shopify if products).
- [ ] **Contracts/intake forms** for any 1-on-1 work — drafted by an attorney once, reused.
- [ ] **Customer service SOP** — who answers email, response time target.
